Fried Rice

📖 History of Fried Rice

Fried rice is a dish made from cooked rice that has been stir-fried in a wok or large pan with oil or fat. It is often mixed with other ingredients such as vegetables, eggs, or meat.

Origin: Fried rice has its roots in China, where it was developed as a way to use up leftover cooked rice. It later spread to other East Asian countries, each adding their own twist.

Cultural Significance: Fried rice is a staple dish in many Asian cuisines and is often served at home and in restaurants. It is versatile and can be customized to suit various tastes and dietary preferences.

Modern Variations: Today, you can find many creative versions of fried rice, from simple egg fried rice to more elaborate dishes with seafood, vegetables, and various seasonings!

Ingredients:

  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 2 medium scallions
  • 2 tablespoons plus 1 teaspoon vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 cup frozen peas and carrots (do not thaw)
  • 4 cups cold cooked brown or white rice (see Recipe Notes), preferably medium grain
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce or tamari
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more as needed
  • 3/4 teaspoon ground white pepper, plus more as needed
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3/4 teaspoon toasted sesame oil

Instructions:

  1. Mince 3 garlic cloves. Thinly slice 2 medium scallions crosswise on a slight diagonal, keeping the white parts separate from the light and dark green parts.
  2. Heat 2 tablespoons of the vegetable oil in a large wok or nonstick frying pan over medium-high heat until shimmering. Add the garlic, scallion whites, and 1 cup frozen peas and carrots. Stir-fry until the carrots and peas are thawed, about 2 minutes.
  3. Add 4 cups cold cooked rice, breaking up any clumps of rice with your fingers as you add it in. Toss to combine with the peas and carrots. Add 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, and 3/4 teaspoon ground white pepper. Stir to combine.
  4. Push the fried rice to one side of the pan. Add the remaining 1 teaspoon vegetable oil to the now-empty side of the pan, then crack 2 large eggs into the oil. Scramble into fluffy curds until almost set.
  5. Push the fried rice to one side of the pan. Add the remaining 1 teaspoon vegetable oil to the now-empty side of the pan, then crack 2 large eggs into the oil. Scramble into fluffy curds until almost set.
  6. Drizzle 3/4 teaspoon toasted sesame oil over the fried rice and eggs. Toss to combine, breaking up any large pieces of egg as you mix. Taste and adjust seasoning with more soy sauce, salt, or white pepper as needed. Serve immediately.

    7. Recipe Notes:

  7. Cook: Cook 1 1/3 cups raw white or brown rice to get 4 cups cooked rice.
  8. Make ahead: Cook the rice up to 1 day ahead. Spread it out on a rimmed baking sheet in a thin layer, then refrigerate.
  9. Storage: Fried rice can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 4 days or frozen for up to 2 months.
